Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose – A Fearless Patriot

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose was one of India’s greatest freedom fighters. Born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, he became known for his bold and unyielding commitment to India’s independence. He famously disagreed with the Indian National Congress's moderate approach and instead advocated for complete and immediate independence. During World War II, he formed the Indian National Army (INA) to fight against British rule and sought support from Germany and Japan. His slogan, "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom," inspired millions.

Netaji's mysterious death in 1945 remains a subject of debate, but his legacy as a patriot who was willing to sacrifice everything for his country continues to live on.
